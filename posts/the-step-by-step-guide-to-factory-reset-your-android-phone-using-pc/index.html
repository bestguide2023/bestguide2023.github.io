Q1: What is a factory reset?

A1: A factory reset is a process that wipes all the data on your Android device and restores it to its original factory settings. This can be helpful if your device is malfunctioning or if you want to reset all your settings, start over from scratch, or sell your device.

Q2: How do I factory reset my Android phone using a PC?

A2: To factory reset your Android phone using a PC, you will need to download and install the Android SDK (Software Development Kit) on your PC. Once installed, connect your device to your PC using a USB cable. Then, open the Android SDK Manager to run the factory reset.

Q3: What data will I lose when I factory reset my Android phone?

A3: When you factory reset your Android phone, all the system settings, user data, and apps will be erased. This includes any files and photos that you have stored on your device.

Q4: Will I need to re-enter my login credentials after a factory reset?

A4: Yes, if your device has any protected services that require a login (such as Google accounts), you will need to re-enter your credentials after the factory reset.

Q5: Will factory reset help with virus removal?

A5: Factory reset is a good way to remove any viruses or malware that may be on your device. However, it is important to note that any viruses or malware that may be stored on an external storage device (such as an SD card) will not be removed.
