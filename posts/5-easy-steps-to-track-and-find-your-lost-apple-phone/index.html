5. Understand the Difference Between Lost Mode and Erase Mode#

When you lose your phone, there are two major courses of action you can take: enabling Lost Mode or erasing your device. Lost Mode allows you to lock or track your device and display a contact number on the screen in case it is found. Erasing your device, on the other hand, deletes all the personal data on the phone. It’s important to understand how each of two functions work.

Lost Mode requires your Apple ID and password which can be used to lock your device with a passcode and display a contact number. The phone will keep track of its location and can be used to show the device on a map. This mode does not erase your data.

Erase Mode is enabled through Find My iPhone. It will erase all the settings and data on the device and remove it from your Apple ID. Even if you later find your device, the data on the phone will be gone.

You can switch between Lost Mode and Erase Mode any time, but once you erase your device, the data will not be recoverable. So be sure you understand the distinction between Lost Mode and Erase Mode before making a deleted. Use Lost Mode as a first choice when you lose your phone and Erase Mode as a last resort.
