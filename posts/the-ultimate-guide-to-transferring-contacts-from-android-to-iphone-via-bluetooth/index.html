Transferring contacts from Android to iPhone can be intimidating. But with the use of Bluetooth technology, it’s easy and fast. Here is the ultimate guide to transferring contacts from Android to iPhone via Bluetooth. First, enable Bluetooth on both devices. On Android, open the Settings app, tap Connections, and turn on Bluetooth. On iPhone, open Settings, tap Bluetooth, and toggle the switch to On. Second, put your Android device in pairing mode. On some Android devices, you can do this by going to Settings, then Connections, or Network & Internet, and selecting Bluetooth.

Next, pair your devices. On your Android device, tap the device name of your iPhone when it appears in the list of available devices. On your iPhone, tap the device name of your Android device to pair them. Both devices will need to accept the pairing request before they can communicate with each other. Finally, transfer the contacts. On your Android device, open the Contacts app and select the contacts you wish to transfer. Then, tap the three-dot menu icon in the top right corner and choose Share. Select Bluetooth as your sharing method and tap the device name of your iPhone. The contacts you selected will then transfer to your iPhone.
