1. Overview of Windows Phone and Android Phone#

Technology evolves rapidly and many of us use multiple devices. When you switch from a Windows Phone to an Android phone, you may want to transfer your contacts from one device to the other. You can use Bluetooth to easily do that.

Firstly, make sure that your Bluetooth on both devices is turned on. On both devices, go into the settings and look for the Bluetooth section. Select your target device from the list of available devices and confirm the connection.

Once the connection has been established, open Contacts or People on your Windows Phone. Select the contacts you want to transfer and tap the three-dot menu. From the menu, select Share and choose Bluetooth as the sharing method. Then select the target device from the list.

The contacts will then be shipped to the target device and you will be asked to confirm the import. Once you do that, your selected contacts will be imported into the people or contacts app on the Android phone.

By following these steps, you’ll be able to easily transfer contacts from your Windows Phone to your Android phone via Bluetooth. No USB cables or online connections are required, and the process is fast and secure.
