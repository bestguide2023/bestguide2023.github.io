2. Accessing the Screenshot Tool#

Taking screenshots on your Android Moto G can seem complicated at first, but it’s actually very simple. Here are 7 easy steps to access the Screenshot tool:

  3. Quickly access your screenshot in the notification bar.

  4. You can also view the screenshot from the Gallery app. Just open the Gallery app, locate and open the Screenshots folder.

  5. If you want share the screenshot, press and hold the screenshot and you will be presented with sharing options.

  6. To delete the screenshot, press and hold the screenshot and select the Delete icon.

  7. Alternatively, you can delete the screenshot from your Gallery app. Just open the Screenshots folder and delete the screenshot you want to get rid of.

Q1: How can I take screenshots on my Android Moto G?

A1: Taking a screenshot on an Android Moto G is easy. You can follow these seven easy steps: press and hold the ‘Power’ and ‘Volume Down’ buttons simultaneously; wait for the screen to flash; open the ‘Gallery’ app; go to ‘Albums’, then ‘Screenshots’; select the screenshot from the list of recent captures; tap on the three vertical dots at the top right corner of the screen; and lastly, select the ‘Share’ button to save or share the file.

Q2: How do I view my screenshots?

A2: After taking the screenshot, you can view the image in the ‘Gallery’ app. Go to the ‘Albums’ tab and select ‘Screenshots’. All the screenshots you’ve taken will be listed there.

Q3: Where does the screenshot get saved?

A3: The screenshots are saved in the internal storage of your device. To access them, open the ‘Gallery’ app, then go to ‘Albums’ and select ‘Screenshots’. The image will be saved in the ‘Downloads’ folder.

Q4: What file type are screenshots saved in?

A4: Screenshots are typically saved in the .jpg and .png file formats.

Q5: What buttons do I need to press to take a screenshot?

A5: To take a screenshot, you need to press and hold the ‘Power’ and ‘Volume Down’ buttons simultaneously. After pressing the buttons, the screen will flash, confirming the capture.
